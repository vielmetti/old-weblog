Rather than being tied to a single repository owned by a single maintainer who accepts or rejects patches at their whims, you can easily fork an entire project in github and then work on it on your own. Popcorn recipe, oil ripples, and Bénard-Marangoni convection

Popcorn canola oil popping corn Heat a sturdy deep pot with a lid to the same temperature you'd use for low temperature sauteing; on our stove it's 6 of 10.  When the pot is properly warmed up, pour a bit of oil on the bottom (about a tablespoon or a little more) and bring that up to heat.  I know it's at the right temperature when the thin layer of oil starts to form standing waves, ridges, or ripples.  If it starts to smoke, it's too hot; if it's a completely flat layer of oil, it's too cold. »

Radish salad with soy and sesame dressing

This is the simplest possible thing to make with a bunch of radishes fresh from farmers market ($1.25 this week). one bunch radishes vinegar soy sauce sesame oil Wash and trim the radishes.  Slice thin, put in a bowl.  Add vinegar (I used white vinegar, but rice vinegar would do well), soy sauce, and a little sesame oil; you're after enough to moisten, not to cover.  Let sit a few minutes before eating.  Put the leftovers, if there are any, in the fridge; eat them while writing at night. »
