Popcorn recipe, oil ripples, and Bénard-Marangoni convection

Popcorn canola oil popping corn Heat a sturdy deep pot with a lid to the same temperature you'd use for low temperature sauteing; on our stove it's 6 of 10.  When the pot is properly warmed up, pour a bit of oil on the bottom (about a tablespoon or a little more) and bring that up to heat.  I know it's at the right temperature when the thin layer of oil starts to form standing waves, ridges, or ripples.  If it starts to smoke, it's too hot; if it's a completely flat layer of oil, it's too cold. »

Radish salad with soy and sesame dressing

How to freeze berries

Wash, drain, then spread in a single layer on a cookie sheet.  Freeze until solid and then use a spatula to carefully scrape them (whole) into freezer bags or tubs. Label, carefully.  "Beans, do not open until February".  So that when it's February, you'll have something to look forward to, and so that the fridge monsters don't eat them before then. »
