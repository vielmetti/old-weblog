Arborwiki is now hosted on Localwiki

The latest update for Arborwiki is that the database is now hosted on the Localwiki central platform. This gives us updated maps, current software, and additional central hosting support. The cut over was on May 1, 2014. All of the old Arborwiki URLs still work; you can get to the home page at http://localwiki.net/ann-arbor or at http://arborwiki.org . We fixed a temporary problem where old URLs with a /city/ prefix stopped working. »

Michigan Wikipedians - U of Michigan student Wikipedia association

Michigan Wikipedians is a University of Michigan student association. Michigan Wikipedians (or MWiki) is a Wikipedia student organization at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. The group was the first student Wikipedia club in the United States. The club is open to all students and faculty of the University of Michigan, as well as community members who are interested in Wikipedia. It meets every Monday at 8:00PM at the Language Resource Center in the basement of North Quadrangle. »

Locality vs notability

  Localwiki In Wikipedia, every article is measured against a broad global sense of "notability". An individual or a topic has to be notable and newsworthy enough to be included, and though the standards vary there is some global consensus to what it means. If an article doesn't meet the grade, it's subject to deletion.   On a Localwiki, every article is measured against an individual sense of "locality". »

ArborWiki is moving to a Localwiki platform; what to expect

Arborwiki is a local encyclopedia about the Ann Arbor area that anyone can edit. Up til now, it's been running on the same Mediawiki software that powers Wikipedia, but we're in the process of moving it to a new Localwiki instance. Localwiki is wiki software designed especially for using in city wikis. It's based off of ideas learned from DavisWiki, a very successful wiki based in Davis, California. The Localwiki software is especially good at handling maps, and so it should be that much easier to map out the streets and roads of Ann Arbor and surrounding Washtenaw County. »
