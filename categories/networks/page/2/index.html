When did the internet go commercial?

A friend asks, "when did the Internet go commercial"?  Here's something of a timeline, incomplete, but footnoted as best I can. 1987: UUNET launches as a non-profit providing Usenet feeds 1989: Software Tool and Die in Boston, MA goes online as the first commercial Internet service provider. 1990: UUNET, now for profit, launches Alternet, an IP network that accepts commercial traffic. 1991: August, the Commercial Internet Exchange (CIX) connects CERFnet, PSInet, and Alternet 1991: MSEN goes online as one of the first commercial ISPs in Michigan.  1993: ANS CO+RE joins CIX The paper Internet Cost Structures and Interconnection Agreements goes into (too much) details about how the money flows between commerical networks; it is a viewpoint from 1995 of the Internet then.  But basically I'd put a date as August 1991 when you could buy an internet connection from one commercial provider and use it to send data to a customer on another commercial provider and not violate any acceptable use politics along the way. »
