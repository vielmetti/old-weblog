Detroit Sound Conservancy: Greystone Jazz rescue

The Detroit Sound Conservancy went to the vacant Book Building in Detroit on July 7, 2015 to salvage the collection of the defunct Graystone International Jazz Museum. There’s a nice writeup in MLive, Graystone jazz artifacts, forgotten in Detroit skyscraper, now being preserved. Photographer Doug Coombe went along to document the effort with a photo set on Facebook. “We knew that things were bad,” said DSC’s Founder and President Carleton Gholz about the neglected collection, but Gholz didn’t know how bad until he discovered many of the former museum’s records were broken and strewn across the floor. »
