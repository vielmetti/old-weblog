expertise encapsulated into out of date memory banks

In the case of experts “writing down what they know”, and incorporating that data into an expert system or knowledge base. One of the challenges is expecting that the expert can write down everything that they know, all at once, in such a way that there’s no internal inconsistencies. The world usually works in such a way that it’s changing fast enough that an expert with a written memory bank of a certain size is always holding as “true” in that memory bank something that is demonstrably false, just because the world shifts out from under them. »

Michigan Emergency Incidents and overnight storm report

Michigan Emergency Incidents is a news service that covers emergency dispatch around the state of Michigan. Members of a closed Facebook group post call-outs of emergency services operations, and there’s generally follow up from other members to note incidents in progress or news stories written from the scene. All information contained in MEI posts is to come from received radio transmissions heard directly from radios / scanners or via an online broadcast, verified reliable sources, or first hand knowledge (ie you are on scene). »

Blogrolls

A blogroll is a list of other weblogs that the writer links to, in the hopes that they will get a link back in return. It’s one way to establish yourself as part of a community of writers. The practice of keeping and maintaining a blogroll is completely gone, as self-linking networks like Facebook and Twitter have obviated the need for explicitly calling out those linkages. Our self-updating feeds simply stream a continuous loop of content into our willing eyeballs, interspersed with as many advertisements as we will stand. »
