Deep web

I think this whole “deep web” thing is mis-stated for the most part. Since forever there have been Internet-based services that have been unreachable to the public unless you had some kind of specialized access. Private internal networks are the norm at companies of any size. Calling that the “hidden web”, “deep web” is non-sensical in that it’s not really hidden to anyone who has proper access to it - it’s just that you (and Google) don’t and won’t have that access. »

gqrx-scan is a Perl script that drives the remote control of the gqrx software defined radio receiver to turn it into a scanner and automated recording tool. The GQRX remote control protocol is well documented and fairly simple, which means that the bulk of the Perl script has to do with setting and getting options. You can drive your scanner from the GQRX configuration file, or you can monitor a specific band or range of frequencies. »

wakatime, an automatic time tracker for programmers

Wakatime tracks your time if you’re a programmer. It does this by integrating into your favorite text editor (including vim) and your favorite source code maintainence system (including Github) and then matching time spent editing with checkins into your system. It collects stats behind the scenes and turns everything into a shiny dashboard. “Fitbit for programmers” is the tag line. That which is measured, improves. I don’t know if wakatime will improve your writing, or your coding, or your general state of mind. »

In the case of experts “writing down what they know”, and incorporating that data into an expert system or knowledge base. One of the challenges is expecting that the expert can write down everything that they know, all at once, in such a way that there’s no internal inconsistencies. The world usually works in such a way that it’s changing fast enough that an expert with a written memory bank of a certain size is always holding as “true” in that memory bank something that is demonstrably false, just because the world shifts out from under them. »
