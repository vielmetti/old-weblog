A salute to Oulipo, providing inspiration since my keyboard is missing a key and I wonder if anyone will notice if I don't use it

A France-based school of writing called "Oulipo" embraces a use of constraints in formal writing in order to obtain an interesting result. A most famous case is an entire novel written missing a letter "e", and a lot of work done on writing long, elaborate palindromes. By work's end, you are just amazed it's possible to meet constraints of a stringent writing format. A constraint I am working under now is an absence of an "h" key on my keyboard. »
