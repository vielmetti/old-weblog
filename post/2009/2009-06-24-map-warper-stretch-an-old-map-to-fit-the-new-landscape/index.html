Map Warper: stretch an old map to fit the new landscape

http://www.vizworld.com/2009/06/testing-map-warper-beta/

Maitri has a great write-up of Map Warper, a tool for taking old maps and stretching them (rectifying them) to match another base map. 

Tim Waters has developed an open source tool called Map Warper (Beta) into which you can upload a scanned map, georectify it and save it out as an image or a map layer.  As a former resident of New Orleans, where a transparent recovery and historic preservation are daily uphill battles, this tool can be invaluable in saving and sharing ancient and new maps of the cityâ€™s original development and re-development since Hurricane Katrina and The Flood.  The following shows how, in under ten minutes, I georectified an 1895 map of the French Quarter and Garden District of New Orleans and exported it to Google Maps.

Worth a look, especially if the bits of history you have collected include maps that don't quite line up with the current street maps.
