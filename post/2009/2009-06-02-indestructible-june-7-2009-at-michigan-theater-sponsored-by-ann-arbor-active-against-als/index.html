"Indestructible", June 7 2009 at Michigan Theater - sponsored by Ann Arbor Active Against ALS

from the Burns Park mailing list

SUNDAY JUNE 7th
2:00 at the Michigan Theater
Award-Winning Film Indestructible and panel discussion

Ann Arbor Active Against ALS (A2A3) is sponsoring a screening of the award-winning film “Indestructible” to raise awareness about ALS (Lou Gehrig’s Disease) and raise funds for research.   

“Indestructible” was created by Ben Byer, a young filmmaker who chronicles his experience with ALS (Lou Gehrig's Disease) and his search for a treatment across six different countries. Winner of the Jury Prize for Best Feature Documentary at the 2007 Cinequest Film Festival, "Indestructible" provides an expansive portrait of the human drama behind one of modern medicine’s greatest challenges. Following the film, there will be a roundtable discussion on the state of research on ALS with brief presentations by Eva Feldman, MD, Ph.D., Russell N. DeJong Professor of Neurology, Program for Neurology Research & Discovery, University of Michigan, and John McCarty, Ph.D., Director, Therapeutic Investigations, ALS Therapy Development Institute, with time for audience questions.  

The film is open to the public, with a suggested donation of $10 per person. All proceeds will be donated to ALS research centers that are aggressively seeking a cure. For more information and to view a trailer of the movie, please visit: www.a2a3.org <http://www.a2a3.org> . Thank you!
