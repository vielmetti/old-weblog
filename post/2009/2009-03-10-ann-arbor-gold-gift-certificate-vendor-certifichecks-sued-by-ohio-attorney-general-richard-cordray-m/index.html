Ann Arbor Gold gift certificate vendor Certifichecks sued by Ohio Attorney General Richard Cordray, MSU '81

Mar 10, 2009

Previously: "Ann Arbor Gold Turns To Lead"

The Chamber was recently informed that CertifiChecks, the company that administered the Ann Arbor Gold program, has gone out of business.



The Ohio Attorney General, Richard Cordray, has sued Ann Arbor Gold vendor Certifichecks in Dayton, OH for violation of Ohio’s Consumer Sales Practices Act in conjunction with them shutting down redemptions of gift certificates from the Ann Arbor Chamber of Commerce and 100s of other Chambers and civic organizations around the country.

The Dayton Business Journal has this reporting, from reporter 'jdirr'

E-mail jdirr@bizjournals.com. Call (937) 528-4424.

Ohio Attorney General Richard Cordray is suing the defunct gift certificate company CertifiChecks Inc. for misleading consumers.

Cordray filed the lawsuit Monday in Montgomery County Court of Common Pleas.


Cordray has issued this press release from the Ohio Attorney General's office:

Attorney General Cordray asks the court to hold CertifiChecks responsible for reimbursing consumers and to declare that the company’s actions violate the law. Cordray also asks the court to impose upon CertifiChecks a civil penalty of at least $25,000 for its violations.

Consumers who lost money to CertifiChecks should file a complaint with Attorney General Cordray’s Office at SpeakOutOhio.gov or by calling (800) 282-0515.
