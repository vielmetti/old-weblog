Ann Arbor Gold gift certificates being redeemed by the Ann Arbor Area chamber; Certifichecks Chapter 7 bankruptcy in progress

Ann Arbor Gold gift certificates being redeemed by the Ann Arbor Area chamber; Certifichecks Chapter 7 bankruptcy in progress

The Ann Arbor Chronicle reported that the Ann Arbor Gold gift certificates will be redeemed by the Chamber of Commerce:

The Ann Arbor Area Chamber of Commerce now plans to use its own funds to reimburse people who purchased Ann Arbor Gold certificates. Earlier this year, the company that administers the program, CertifiChecks, filed for bankruptcy.


An April 15 story from the AP notes that the bankruptcy filing has gone through in Dayton, OH (via the Newark Advocate):

CertifiChecks Inc., which abruptly closed in February, filed for Chapter 7 Monday in U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of Ohio. CertifiChecks listed assets of $1 million in personal property that included funds in savings and checking accounts in the filing. It also listed liabilities of $460,000.

A recorded message at the court clerk's number for automated case information said a meeting of the company's creditors was scheduled for June 10.

Here's what the Ohio Attorney General has to say about the matter:

On April 13, Dayton-based gift certificate company CertifiChecks, Inc. filed for Chapter 7 bankruptcy in the United States Bankruptcy Court Southern District of Ohio, Dayton Division.

You must file your Proof of Claim with the bankruptcy court by September 8, 2009.  Filing a Proof of Claim tells the court that you are a “creditor” (which means that money may be due to you).  It also helps to ensure that you will receive important updates about the bankruptcy.  You can even fill out a Proof of Claim form electronically.

Notice of Chapter 7 Bankruptcy – United States Bankruptcy Court

I'll assume that chambers of commerce who redeem their members gift certificates will take a loss, and that they will bundle up all of the certificates redeemed for the bankruptcy court; I have no idea how much of this "money" is recoverable, but Chapter 7 means the organization is liquidating not to return to business.
