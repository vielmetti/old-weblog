Baked Polenta: "no-stir polenta sounds like no-water soup - possible but unlikely"

The family recipes for polenta all involve stirring for a long time, but I didn't want to do that.  This alternative approach showed up in a search for the delicious tag polenta, from the blog Hungry Cravings - the so called Cheater Method Polenta.

The critics were skeptical:

Vaguery: @vielmetti no-stir polenta sounds like no-water soup. Possible, but unlikely. Good luck!

but it turned out fine - no stir is an exaggeration, more like one or two stirs but not more than that.


Baked Polenta
adapted from a recipe by Lucy Vaserfirer.

1 cup polenta
4 cups boiling water
salt
pepper

preheat oven to 425 degrees.
pour polenta into an 8x8 baking pan.
pour boiling water on top, stir.
season with salt and pepper.
put in the oven to cook.
stir a couple of times, take it out when it's done, about 40 minutes.

You end up with something delicious - it's not creamy like well-stirred polenta, but it comes out of the pan in lovely squares ready for sauce.  If anything it's more like a one-step recipe for baked leftover polenta. Serve with a sauce on top.

The original recipe has more spices (hot pepper flakes) and more substance (cheese, pepperoni), but I thought I'd bring it back to basics for the start.
