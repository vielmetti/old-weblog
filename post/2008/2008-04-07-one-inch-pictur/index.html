7 April 2008

One inch picture frame

I'm rereading Anne Lamott's bird by bird, and am inspired by the chapter on short assignments and on shitty first drafts to write some code.

Crontab: 00,20,40 * * * * getoutagemap

Getoutagemap: #!/bin/sh curl $map > $map.$now pdf2png $map.$now > $png.$now
For where in $(ziplist)
Do
Getpixel.$png.$now $(pixeldata $where) |
Pixdata2csv >> outages.csv Done

Much better to have dte report the table of areas with power outages directly as a csv than to have to reconstruct it from pixel color values on a map, but we cope with the civic infrastructure we have, not the civic infrastructure we want.

I'm guessing on the ability to render a pdf to a png from the command line, and the existence of a tool to extract the value of a pixel at a certain place from a png. Everything else is a table look up.

The output goal looks like

5 April 08 12:11am,48104,1500

$now,$zip,$subscribers

Suitable for excel and then powerpoint and people with suits.
