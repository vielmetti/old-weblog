#atlgas: real time reporting on gasoline availability in Atlanta, using Twitter

For a bunch of reasons which I don't completely understand, there are gasoline shortages in the Atlanta area.

The net is responding by using the #atlgas tag on Twitter to coordinate real time information about the location and availability of gasoline. 

To look at it the easiest way is through the twitter search for #atlgas.  Some recent examples -

wheelmom: #atlgas BP at I-85 and Clairmont. $4.39(!) Barely a wait.

        wheelmom: #atlgas Gas as QT I-85 and Pleasantdale Rd. near I-285 interchange. $3.99. Lines about 15 cars deep at each driveway entrance.

        jinah: #atlgas Hemphill/14th BP had all grades at 8 PM starting at $4.29. Less than 5 min wait.  

        esmathis: QT n DeKalb mall now out. #atlgas

The Web 1.0 of gas price reporting is very efficient, using reports from credit receipts of fleet vehicles to give you next day pricing nation wide.  The Web 2.0 version of this is GasBuddy, which lets you pinpoint pricing as part of a social network.  Neither of these two services have the notion that a station might not have any gas, or that it might have a line, and that the length of the line is part of the price of the gas.  so twitter adaptively fills in the blank.
