Buy Michigan blueberries - retail, wholesale, photography and u-pick

It's blueberry season, and so one of the things you want to do is buy some berries. Here's how.

For stock photography, Featurepix has a photo of fresh Michigan blueberries on a bush; the original photo is $4. Another wild blueberries on the bush photo from istockphoto is 10 credits (quatloos? rubles? I don't know the exchange rate).

At retail, Busch's in Ann Arbor is currently selling them for $14.99 for a 5 pound box, or $1.99 per pint (on sale from $2.99/pint).

Russell's Blueberry Farm in Freeland, MI (near Saginaw) has posted 2007 prices of $2.10/lb wholesale, and $1.45/lb u-pick. Call for 2008 pricing.

Reenders Blueberry Farm in Grand Haven has a page on Merchant Circle where potential customers are asking questions like "We are interested in purchasing around 500 bluberry lugs, 36x16x7. If you sell these, we would like a quote. Thanks". The web site for the Reenders Blueberry Farm lists 2008 prices, 10 pounds for $21.50.

Blueberrry Lane Farms in Otter Lake, MI, northeast of Flint, MI has U-Pick $1.80/lb, and 10 lb pre-pick with box $35.00. Place your pre-pick orders 1 week in advance. The farm has been in blueberry production since 1949 and the Elliott blueberry was developed there.

Wild berries in the UP should be good near the Sleeper Lake Fire area; stay near Paradise, Michigan or Newberry, Michigan or Pine Stump Junction, Michigan or McMillan, Michigan (home of the Generic Motel, "clean, cheap"). Call ahead for when the berries are ripe and check before going onto private property. Bridge toll on the Mackinac Bridge, $3.00.
