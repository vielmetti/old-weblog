"The Story of the Apple", Barrie Juniper on the history and evolution of apples

The Story of the Apple by Barrie Juniper (ISBN 0881927848) is a 2006 book which traces the evolution of the apple through time and tries to answer the question why of all things natural evolution resulted in a widely palatable fruit, where the natural variation of apple varieties gives only about 1% of the total being tasty. 

Barrie's daughter, Sarah Juniper, runs The Apple Factor, an antique apple consultancy in the UK.

There's an extensive apple cultivation at Cornell University, and the Cornell Fruit gateway ... antique varieties and rare and breeding.

  • Fruit trees - Apples, pears, peaches, plums, cherries, etc.
  • Grapes - Juice-, wine- & table-grape viticulture, enology.
  • Berries - Strawberries, blueberries, brambles (blackberries and raspberries), cranberries, currants and gooseberries.

Closer to home, we get our apples from Wasem's Fruit Farm at the Ann Arbor Farmers Market.  Ask for a bag of "mixed apple sauce apples, seconds" and you get a nice variety for making homemade apple sauce.
