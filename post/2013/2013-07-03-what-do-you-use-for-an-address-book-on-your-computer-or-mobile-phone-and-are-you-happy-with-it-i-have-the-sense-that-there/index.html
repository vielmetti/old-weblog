What do you use for an address book on your computer or mobile phone?

What do you use for an address book on your computer or mobile phone, and are you happy with it? I have the sense that there could be much better tools for collecting useful scraps of information about your correspondents, but nothing I've seen recently answers the nagging feeling that we could be doing better with this.

The two apps that I have seen that do something different in this space are Evernote Hello and Brewster.

Evernote Hello works cross-platform to help you log contacts through the Evernote platform, so that your contact records can be searched through the normal Evernote system. I have it running on my Android phone, and I use it from time to time (but not religiously) to be mindful of when and where I meet folks. It really wants you to use photography to record the moment, and I'm always hesitant to pull out my camera and take a snapshot of people.

Brewster is a smart address book for iPhone that watches your communications and helps nudge you to reconnect with people who you've been out of touch with. I haven't used it, but reviews have been good. I don't know their plans to make an Android version.

Mostly I use Google Contacts and the Android People application to keep track, however unevenly, of connections. The biggest plus is that they sync perfectly. The biggest minus is that they are very weak at location-specific searching, so I don't have an easy way to construct a list of "everyone with an address within 50 miles of Marquette, MI" - a very reasonable desire to be able to do while traveling, but not something directly supported.
