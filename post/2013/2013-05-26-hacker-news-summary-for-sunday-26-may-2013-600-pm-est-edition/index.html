Hacker News summary for Sunday 26 May 2013, 6:00 p.m. EST edition

Hacker News summary for Sunday 26 May 2013, 6:00 p.m. EST edition

Compiled hastily, here's a summary of today's Hacker News top 10. Based solely on the top 10, it's a slow news day.

1. "If you try to get several regular polygons to meet snugly at a point in the plane, what’s the most sides any of the polygons can have? The answer is 42." (Azimuth Project) Some observations of mathematical questions for which the answer is 42 or a multiple thereof, in time for Towel Day.

2. He took the $300k bonus (Cliff Moon), a follow up to yesterday's account of someone who didn't (Tom Preston-Warner). It turned out well for both of them.

3. "How far out is it possible to overhang blocks from the edge of a table without them toppling?" The answer, surprisingly, is infinitely far. (DataGenetics)

4. Paypal has a cross-site scripting (XSS) vulnerability. (Robert Kugler, Full Disclosure) Paypal pays bug bounties for people who find problems, but only to people over 18; the researcher who located this defect is a 17 yr old German student.
5. "In 2012, under threat from fundamentalist rebels, a team of archivists, librarians, and couriers evacuated an irreplaceable trove of manuscripts from Timbuktu at great personal risk." Help save a library in exile, there's more work to be done. (Kickstarter)
6. Rustboot is a tiny 32 bit kernel written in Rust. (Charlie Somerville, Github) Rust is a curly-brace, block-structured expression language oriented toward concerns of “programming in the large”. (Rust)
7. Cameron D'Ambrosio has been locked up without bail for weeks – facing terrorism charges and 20 years in prison – all for something he posted on Facebook after the Boston Marathon bombing. (Fight For The Future)
8. People who have enough savings to be unafraid of being fired are happier at work than those who depend on every paycheck (Mr. Money Moustache)
9. Twitter is a big deal because "it's a new messaging protocol, where you don't specify the recipients. New protocols are rare." (Paul Graham, 2009)
10. BetterPlace, a "fat startup" that tried to "change the world" about how people drive, has failed. (Eytan Levit) "Their biggest assumption was that people are willing to compromise on a less convenient/more risky car solution just because it’s eco-friendly."
