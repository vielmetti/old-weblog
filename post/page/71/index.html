How to freeze berries

Wash, drain, then spread in a single layer on a cookie sheet.  Freeze until solid and then use a spatula to carefully scrape them (whole) into freezer bags or tubs. Label, carefully.  "Beans, do not open until February".  So that when it's February, you'll have something to look forward to, and so that the fridge monsters don't eat them before then. »

As seen in the Duke University news: Little Teeth Suggest Big Jump in Primate Timeline DURHAM, NC -- Tiny fossilized teeth excavated from an Indian open-pit coal mine could be the oldest Asian remains ever found of anthropoids, the primate lineage of today's monkeys, apes and humans, say researchers from Duke University and the Indian Institute of Technology. Just 9-thousandths of a square inch in size, the teeth are about 54.5 million years old and suggest these early primates were no larger than modern dwarf lemurs weighing about 2 to 3 ounces. »

Supermemo: repetition is the very soul of the net

I wrote about SuperMemo some months back, but if I've told you once, I've told you 146 times: repetition is the very soul of the net. SuperMemo is a piece of software from P A Wozniak, originally developed in 1987 as a DOS program for learning languages.  Wozniak, a student of molecular biology, was frustrated by the amount of knowledge he lost after cramming for an exam, and worked to develop methods for improving long term memory.  His 1992 masters thesis, Optimization of Learning, covered the early work on Supermemo and a discussion of spaced repetition in learning. »
