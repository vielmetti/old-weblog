Old code and old tools

One of the problems with old codebases is that they are missing modern tooling for automated builds, automated testing, and the like. A second problem with old code is that the maintainers (absent modern tooling) may not know or recognize what dependencies they have. If an old package is not mostly self-contained, it’s likely to have dependencies on other old code. This makes it hard to build, as those older dependencies have either gone offline or have mutated so that the old package doesn’t work with the new releases. »

You can drive your scanner from the GQRX configuration file, or you can monitor a specific band or range of frequencies. »

wakatime, an automatic time tracker for programmers

Wakatime tracks your time if you’re a programmer. It does this by integrating into your favorite text editor (including vim) and your favorite source code maintainence system (including Github) and then matching time spent editing with checkins into your system. It collects stats behind the scenes and turns everything into a shiny dashboard. “Fitbit for programmers” is the tag line. That which is measured, improves. I don’t know if wakatime will improve your writing, or your coding, or your general state of mind. »
