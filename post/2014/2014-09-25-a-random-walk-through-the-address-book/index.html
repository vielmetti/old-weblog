A random walk through the address book

25 September 2014

A random walk through the address book

Did I just complain about address books? Well let me complain again.

My addressbook asks for a random() function. It needs to surprise me from time to time, showing me individuals who aren't going to show up in a search, but who nonetheless are worthy of remembering right now.

My addressbook needs a search by location function, so that I can pan and zoom over geography and figure out who I know where and who's nearby enough but not in the same city. I know this is possible, but I haven't seen it done either on my phone or on the web.

My addressbook wants to have better links between people, so that I can surf sideways among relationships. It wants to vacuum up all sorts of material from my inbox and then present it intelligently so that I can ask it things.

My addressbook is in dire need of cleaning up, as connections go stale and email addresses change and memories fade.

Frankly, something needs to play the role of social secretary, nudging me to remember things, making lists of who should sit next to whom and who doesn't get invited, helping me figure out how to get the call through. I'm afraid that nothing I've ever touched that helps me keep track of people has ever done anything close to what I imagine a skilled person can do in that role.
