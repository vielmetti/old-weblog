Appeal of FOIA 14-315, redaction of names and email addresses

Update: The list below is of individuals and organizations that have requested the Absentee Voter file (AV file) for the most recent Ann Arbor election.

The file includes the full names and addresses of individuals who have requested absentee ballots, as well as the status of the ballot in the current election (whether it has been voted yet or not). Political campaigns use this information to identify likely voters for campaign mailings. The information is very personal, in the sense that it includes full names including middle names and home addresses.

The City of Ann Arbor redacted a request for the names of individuals who have asked for this personal information, citing personal privacy issues in their arbitrary redaction of names seen below.

--

Steve,

This is an appeal of FOIA 14-315.

I appeal all redactions in this document.

Some names and email addresses in the document are redacted, but others are not. This practice is arbitrary and is specifically prohibited by statute.

In addition, the records have been previously released to others; see e.g. the email copied below from Matt Morrow on August 2. Disclosure of public records to some individuals but not others is similarly arbitrary and is in violation of statute.

The city's ongoing arbitrary policy of redaction, as previously noted by Dave Askins of the Ann Arbor Chronicle, is a practice which is unduly expensive and time-consuming for both citizens and staff.

thank you

Edward Vielmetti, Ann Arbor MI +1 734 330 2465
