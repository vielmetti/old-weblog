Power outage map outage at Emera and Central Maine Power (Maine electrical utilities)

November 2, 2014, 5:04 p.m. EST (Ann Arbor, MI): The state of Maine is being hit by winter storms, with more than 1 inch an hour falling across parts of the state. This has led to widespread power outages, as well as outages of the power outage maps provided by Emera and by Central Maine Power, two utility companies that serve the state.


Emera's live outage map is throwing an error at this time. The latest current outage report on their web site is dated 9:26 a.m., with a promise of more up to date info at 4:00 p.m. that has not materialized.

UPDATE 4:49 p.m. November 2, 2014 from Emera:

As of 4:00 p.m. more than 38,000 Emera Maine customers were without power, the result of strong gusty winds and heavy, wet snow weighing down on tree limbs and trees on power lines, especially in Penobscot and Hancock Counties. The utility has declared a system emergency and additional crews are being brought in from our Northern Operating Region and New Brunswick. Crews which had been placed on standby in anticipation of the storm were fully deployed as of this morning.

The Central Maine Power outage viewer is also offline, but at least they have an outage information page as text that describes how 79,310 customers are offline as of 5:12 p.m.

When the power goes out, people use their cell phones to check on system status. It's not acceptable performance by an electrical utility company to not be able to serve the public by providing this information online when it's most needed.

We are calling in additional crews, including crews from Canada, to help our local crews repair storm damage and restore service.
