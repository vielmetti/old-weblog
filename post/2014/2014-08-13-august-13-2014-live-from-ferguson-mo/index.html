August 13, 2014: Live feeds from Ferguson, MO

August 13, 2014: The events in Ferguson, Missouri continue to be tense. This is a developing situation.

2020 CST approx: LRAD noisemakers deployed. All live feeds appear to have gone down.

Live on the scene with Twitter photos and Vine videos is Antonio French, a St Louis alderman; he's been there since the start and is a trusted local voice.

Video feed, live: The Real Alex Jones. Jones is a 9/11 truther and a conspiracy theorist; as of 2035 CST his feed is broadcasting in front of the QT. At approx 2040 the police give an order to disperse immediately; then there is loud noise and what sounds like tear gas.

Video feeds, live: Activist World News Now has the Alex Jones feed plus a second feed.

Television news: Fox 2 News live stream.

Radio interview: Robert Siegal interviews Patricia Bynes @patricilicious about the situation for All Things Considered. 4 minutes.

Public records request: ACLU of Missouri request for incident report for Michael Brown death.

Huffington Post reporter, Washington Post reporter arrested.

Story, Huffington Post.

Ryan Reilly, the justice correspondent for The Huffington Post, and Washington Post reporter Wesley Lowery were working in a McDonald's when a SWAT team suddenly invaded the restaurant. Lowery said that Reilly had been "assaulted" by the officers, and that both of them had been detained. Reilly said that police told them that they were held for "not packing their bags quick enough."

Story, Washington Post.

Wesley Lowery, a reporter for The Washington Post who has been reporting on the unrest in Ferguson, Mo., was detained Wednesday evening by police in Ferguson. He and other reporters were working in a McDonald’s in Ferguson when about half a dozen police officers came into the restaurant, Lowery said. Some officers were in regular uniforms, while others were dressed in riot gear and carrying assault weapons. “It was tense,” Lowery said via telephone from the police station on Wednesday night. “I’ve been afraid several times while reporting on the ground here in Ferguson.”

Scanners: St Louis County scanners on Broadcastify. Many public safety scanners are offline.
