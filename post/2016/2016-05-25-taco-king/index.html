Taco King

The back story on Taco King is that the a2b3 group was here for lunch once about two years ago on the same day that the kitchen was being inspected by the state - and that day the restaurant closed voluntarily to deal with some of its problems on the spot rather than face sanction. We haven’t been back as a group, but I wanted to check out the place to see what I was missing and if anything had obviously changed.

The inspection if you want to see it is on a2docs, as World Call Shop (Taco King) inspections, October 2 2014.

I’m sure it could be a good place to bring a large group for lunch again, if you live with the understanding that not every circumstance is perfect, and some delicious food comes out of kitchens that are not perfectly clean. It doesn’t take much reading of restaurant inspections to know that it’s rare to get a perfect score.

On the down side, loud nonsense television is playing on the TV set. Ads for title loans, and the show is the People’s Court. It’s a noisy background that makes it less fun to eat.

The verdict: it was a bit more expensive than I remembered, even though I had the special. The food was OK but not exceptional; in particular, the lengua taco was not worth the $1.00 upcharge on the daily special, I am a fan of tongue but it wasn’t as articulate as I might expect it to be. And further I think I was charged $1.25 for a drink that was labelled $0.99 on the shelf.

The little things, always! And perhaps the little reasons not to bring a big group.
