Taking a break from Facebook

I’m taking a temporary break from Facebook.

You probably won’t read this message in time to switch any of your normal communciations paths with me; indeed, unless I had told you that I was leaving Facebook idle for a while you wouldn’t notice at all.

I’d prefer it if more people were blogging, and more to the point if I were blogging more. I miss the olden days when you could count on people updating their blogs more than once a month and when it made sense to make the rounds of blogrolls. But I suppose that’s just net.nostalgia for the olden days, the same way you might say that Flickr was better the first few years, and oh we don’t have anything quite like the Napster that we once had.

What we do have is Facebook, and plenty of it. A recent story somewhere that I read somewhere (see, this media makes our brains mush) was that people with Facebook accounts are dying, and that the Facebook we know now will be dominated some time soon by memorials to the dead.

Faced with that grim promise, what to do instead? Twitter is my solace, the endless stacatto chatter punctuated by weird Unicode glyphs, with cryptic tendencies and self-contained non-sequiturs.

I’m taking a break. How can you tell? I’m writing again. I don’t write on Facebook, so much as scroll endlessly and leave little obscure comments made out of mostly-remembered lyrics.
