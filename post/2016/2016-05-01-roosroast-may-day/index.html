May Day at Roos Roast with Burroughs and Polya

An account of the trip; what happened.

Egg and a shot at Roos Roast.

AVOID GRINDING OVER STEAMING POT.

Drinking the espresso shot, with an increasing titration of milk. Reading Twitter. Sunday a.m. tweeting is good.

Anker battery plugged in to 75% Macbook.

Avantgarde Design postcard book, UM Credit Union pen.

Reqeusted “When We Are No More” Abby Smith Rumsey. Deliver to Pittsfield (?!)

What I was thinking at the time.

Reading a Burroughs interview, on his multicolumn notebooks, cut ups, and fold-ins. Writing technology.

I reread How To Solve It when I am looking for work. Does it work? Is there some better text?

Grand Hotel serving sushi, says Crain’s Detroit. Do they also serve smelt? What happened to the smelt runs?

Brian Kerr writes of the intricate ritual of being dependent on electronica, and the daily or more frequent need to recharge batteries.

Abby Smith Rumsey on prserving history in a digtial age;

Pondering that my digital ephemera are dependent on monthly subscriptions, and could fade in a moment.

Cyberspace, remember that, data gloves and VRML and the metaverse and all that? Yeah. There.

Quotations from any book I take with me.
