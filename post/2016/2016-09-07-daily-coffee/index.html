Daily coffee and wifi, September 7, 2016

Sep 7, 2016 #Peoples Food Coop #daily coffee and wifi #Ann Arbor City Council #a2council #Woodbury Club Apartments #South Pond Village #Tom Meloche #Gabriel Radvansky #memory palace #mnemotechnics

Coffee and wifi (double espresso over ice) at the Peoples Food Coop, on North Fourth Ave. The lunch hot bar here is very good and the place is busy.

Last night was Ann Arbor City Council, a marathon session that resulted in grudging approval of site plans for hundreds of new homes and apartments over the opposition of neighbors in adjacent neighborhoods. Woodbury Club Apartments will be on the northeast side of town near Nixon Road and M-14, and South Pond Village will be tucked away off Chalmers behind Arborland but not connected to it. In both cases the votes reflected the split between the Council Party who saw the “yes” vote as obligatory to avoid a lawsuit, and the Anti Party who had few enough votes to be able to vote “no” as a protest vote.

The Apple keynote is today, awaiting news about new shiny toys to see how much less the old shiny toys are worth. Apparently the new Apple watch will have Pokemon Go on board.

The last time I took notes at the coop was July 18, 2015, when I took note of Tom Meloche’s post on the doorway effect, and how our short term memory gets dumped when we cross a threshold. Gabriel Radvansky from Notre Dame is the scientist that Tom remembers. The effect apparently is that going through a door starts a new memory event, and to prepare for that new event and give us a fresh landscape for thinking the short term memory we had been holding gets emptied.

If you have an active imagination you can think of yourself moving through doors without ever really changing your place of sitting. My guess is that not only do you have your current thinking flushed when you leave a room, but the new room (if it’s familiar) pre-loads some thoughts. Construct your mental landscape appropriately and you end up with the memory palace which you can walk through effortlessly in your mind, remembering its details everywhere.
