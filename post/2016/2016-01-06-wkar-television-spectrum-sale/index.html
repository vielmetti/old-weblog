WKAR-TV television spectrum may be up for sale, and the station is at risk

Michigan State University is considering placing the spectrum that it uses for WKAR-TV into an auction that the FCC is running, in which case it might receive upwards of $200 million in exchange for ceasing all of its terrestrial broadcasts.

This strikes me as a penny wise, pound foolish approach. Once the spectrum is gone you can’t get it back, and as a land grant institution should know it’s important to be a good steward of the resources you have been handed not just for the short term but for the long term. Selling off a 50+ year tradition of public broadcasting for a quick buck seems like a bad approach, no matter how appealing a big check would be, especially considering the community’s investment in the station.

I don’t currently have a TV receiver for over the air broadcasts, but my neighbor down the street who does make the trek from Ann Arbor to Lansing to give her thoughts. Her family grew up on WKAR-TV and have been supporters for years, and they rely on over the air television to bring in the signal.

More as I have it; I’m collecting links and news articles at the Lansing Localwiki, at the WKAR-TV page.
