Mac and Goat

Mac and Goat

Mac and Goat calls for goat cheese; we generally use a goat gouda or a goat cheddar, both of which are usually available at the local Trader Joe’s. Feel free to substitute any hard cheese that melts well in your recipe.

The dish also calls for rice pasta, but again feel free to substitute; you’re aiming for something like a rotini which handles the sauce well.

Preheat oven to 375 degrees. Take out a lasagna pan, which is what you’re going to assemble the dish in.

Mac and Goat

  • 1 lb. pasta
  • 1⁄2 lb. goat gouda, goat cheddar, or a nice aged gouda or cheddar
  • 1 large can crushed tomatoes
  • bread crumbs
  • ground nutmeg
  • romano cheese

Boil water for pasta; cook a pound or so of your favorite pasta, we use a rice rotini pasta in this one.

Shred the cheese fine in a food processor or with a hand grater.

When the pasta is done, drain it, and then put about half of it in the lasagna pan. Cover with about half of the cheese and half of the tomatoes. Put the rest of the pasta in, then more tomatoes and more cheese. Top with breadcrumbs and then sprinkle a little romano cheese on top and a few pinches of nutmeg for flavor.

Cook 20-25 minutes, until the top is lightly browned and the tomato sauce is bubbling.
