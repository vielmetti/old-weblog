Focus

Feb 22, 2017

My favorite weblogs are very focused. With them you can count on every post having something narrowly on the theme of the blog, with variety within a certain limit.

This is not a focused weblog, except that I write all of it, and that it reflects my varied interests which don’t lend themselves to a narrow bead on a single topic.

How to reconcile this contradiction? Very well then, I contradict myself.

One way to try to cope with having a wide range of interests is to be honest about it, and to try to limit what you want to say right now. I have tried to do that with my introductory post, but even that seems like it’s been overtaken by events. I’ll refine that statement of purpose to be clear what it is that I could and should write about daily.

A second approach is to be generous in linking out to what I read, so that it’s more obvious by reference what has my attention. So the “radio” section should mention London Shortwave, the RTL-SDR weblog, and KB6NU’s amateur radio blog as three sources - not the only three, but a representative sample of interests.

If I write more, I can go back and unpublish the bits that are off-topic, so that the long-term publication stream fits some more processed and refined notion of self.

A better idea simply is to write more, and to write more often, and to be less telegraphic and more epistolary in my typical communications. 140 characters is not enough, and when I’m tempted to instachirp something, better to get it out in long form than squeezed into a tiny box.

Edward Vielmetti
