For severe weather

Feb 24, 2017

I’m setting aside this evening in anticipation of severe weather. Unseasonable warmth has meant that southeastern Michigan is at risk for a rare February tornado. Ever since the tornado hit Dexter a few years ago, people in the area have had some focus on their storm preparation.

Here’s some ways to prepare.

Best potential for severe storms Friday in the evening and first half of the night, roughly 6PM to midnight. #miwx pic.twitter.com/dtKqAeBqQh

— NWS Detroit (@NWSDetroit) February 23, 2017

First and foremost, charge all of the devices that you care about well in advance.

Twitter is always a good place to watch for news. The hashtag used by weather watchers in Michigan is #miwx , and the National Weather Service Detroit uses the @NWSDetroit twitter feed to keep people informed.

I count on my amateur radio station at home to stay connected to the Skywarn network. Locally this net uses the 145.15 Mhz frequency on the N8DUY repeater, which is located in Scio Township near I-94 and M-14. I spent a little time tonight tuning up and making sure that could get into the repeater OK, and I was successful, thanks to a rig on long-term loan from W8TAM and an antenna that KB6NU helped me build last year.

If the power goes out it’s good to have a backup radio. I have a couple of inexpensive Baofeng rigs that have pretty good battery life and can tune not just to amateur bands but also to broadcast FM and to the weather bands.

If you’re looking for signs of storm damage, the power outage maps provide a 30 minute delayed detailed track of where weather has impacted the power grid. I put together a list of outage maps a few years ago, which is still generally useful; locally, the maps from DTE Energy and Consumers Energy cover most of the Lower Peninsula. Both companies have mobile phone apps that allow you to log outages and view maps.

When weather hits, public safety traffic monitored by scanners can also be of interest. Broadcastify’s list of top feeds is worth checking when dispatchers are handling weather-related calls.

There are several sites that have radar feeds, and of those, I have a fondness for the reporting from the Weather Underground.

I’m missing some AA batteries for a dedicated weather radio that runs on AC power but that can run from batteries; that would be good to sort out before the evening.
