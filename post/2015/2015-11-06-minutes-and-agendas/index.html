6 November 2015

Minutes and agendas

The City of Ann Arbor planned 49 public meetings last month; 5 of them were cancelled. Of the remaining 44, 25 have meeting details posted to the city’s Legistar calendar, and 19 have no record of the meeting beyond its date.

City boards and commissions and committees that routinely meet without publishing minutes or agendas to Legistar include the following:

  • Cable Communications Commission
  • Commission on Disability Issues
  • Council Administration Committee
  • Council Rules Committee
  • Downtown Area Citizens Advisory Council
  • Downtown Development Authority
  • Elizabeth Dean Fund Committee
  • Human Rights Commission
  • Park Advisory Commission Dog Park Subcommittee

Of these, the Downtown Area Citizens Advisory Council and the Downtown Development Authority post minutes to the DDA’s web site. The remainder have no obvious record or trail of their activities, decision making, or agendas.

The omission of minutes from two of these boards is particularly distressing. The Human Rights Commission labors in obscurity, not reporting out any of its meetings to the public, even as it is a focal point for the response to the shooting of Aura Rosser last year. And the Commission on Disability Issues doesn’t manage to produce a record that is accessible by people whose preferred medium is written.

I’ve been through the long, slow, drawn out ritual of asking the city for minutes of meetings before. In 2010, I sent in a series of FOIA requests that prompted the city’s Historic District Commission to produce meeting minutes from that year, after being originally told that those minutes did not exist. The city rectified that problem eventually, and now those minutes routinely show up on time.

Agendas of city meetings are key to understanding when you might want to lift yourself up from your easy chair and get downtown to weigh in on decision making at the city level. Minutes are important as a record of what actually happened. Groups like the Human Rights Commission, which is actively looking to advance its agenda, should be working harder to make sure that the people can see what they are up to. Otherwise you hold a meeting and spend time away from your kids and there’s no record of it - why did you bother?
