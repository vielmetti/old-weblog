18 March 2015

Northern lights in northern Michigan

Photo credit: Ryan Eby, from his aurora photoset on Flickr.


I first got word that there was a chance of northern lights from the Michigan VHF-UHF Society mailing list, an amateur radio group. When the aurora activity is high, the VHF amateur radio bands (2 meter and 6 meter) get increased range, so ham radio operators with this interest keep an alert out.

The Aurora Page at Michigan Tech has a lot of useful links to space weather forecasts.

Alas no sign of or reports of northern lights in Ann Arbor - we don't exactly have a dark sky, but the storm was also not strong enough for any reports this far south.
