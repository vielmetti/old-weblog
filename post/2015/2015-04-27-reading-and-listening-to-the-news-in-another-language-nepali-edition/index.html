Reading and listening to the news in another language, Nepali edition

My command of the Nepali language is extremely limited, Somewhere on a bookshelf somewhere in the house I have this cookbook

and its recipe for "gundruk", a fermented leafy vegetable that I have never tried to make and have not found to eat anywhere here locally.

There is a lot of news from Nepal as a result of the tragic earthquake, and the thought goes to try to read at least a tiny piece of the news in the original language. Learning a new language from scratch is not the easy task, but with news reports you're not really trying to learn the language at all, just to read the text like a "bookbreaker" would read it if they were decoding a secret code.

So, Nepali news, sad all of it. Lots of photos are online, some video. Active message traffic via amateur radio is on 20 meters, which you can monitor at a WebSDR station. Trying to find a good DX monitoring spot for Nepali transmissions.
