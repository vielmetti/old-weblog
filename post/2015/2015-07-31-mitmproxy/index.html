mitmproxy

31 July 2015

mitmproxy

In his An internet x-ray machine for the masses, Paul Ohm writes a lucid introduction to mitmproxy, a man-in-the-middle proxy server that allows you to get between your browser and the Internet and poke at (and change) the traffic going between the two.

http://mitmproxy.org is the home of mitmproxy. It’s the work of Aldo Cortesi and a cast of hundreds of contributors on github at https://github.com/mitmproxy/mitmproxy . I used the OS X binary distribution from the download page. There’s also a nifty Dockerized version on Dockerhub as docker pull mitmproxy/mitmproxy to get you started quickly too.

Man-in-the-middle (or monkey-in-the-middle) systems are not new, of course, and Dug Song’s dsniff package dates to 1999. What we’ve learned in a decade and a half is that security through obscurity vanishes in the face of better tools for looking at network traffic as it’s going by with high level tools for making sense of what’s there.

As ever, all of these tools have both benign and malicious uses. If you can trick someone into using your proxy server for their traffic, all kinds of foolishness can result. Better is the use that you proxy your own traffic and behold all of the cookies, beacons, and ads that sneak into your everyday browsing experience.
