I am eating a sandwich, with a recipe in Github

Twitter is, of course, for telling people that you are eating a sandwich. That’s been the point of the system the whole time - long before selfies, even long before it took pictures at all, you might get a proper narrative of what people are eating for lunch or a snack.

Using the net to keep track of food is an old tradition; I need only point you at the Usenet Cookbook, developed from 1985 to 1987 by Brian Reid, as an archetype. “I had a lettuce and tomato sandwich Saturday with mustard instead of mayonnaise. Better than I would have feared.” But that collection is weak on sandwiches, lending itself more to soups.

To remedy this need I have started a small and pitiful but hopefully growable sandwich recipe collection, using Github as the hosting place of choice. See the vielmetti/sandwich repository for the first start. As it happens, I am clearly not the only person to think this way; herewith some collection of sandwich collections.

Barrie Turner (@bazbt3) came up with roughly the same idea at the same time, and you might see his git sandwiches blog for the proper inspiration for collection development. Thanks to @ellemef for connecting us.

Single sandwiches in git include

  • grilled cheese, from Magnus Hedemark
  • gluten free fried chicken sandwich from Patrick Connelly

and certainly many more.

If you are ill-equipped in your kitchen to make your own sandwiches, this command line tool will order you a sandwich from Jimmy John’s. It comes with a makefile so that you invoke it as sudo make me a sandwich and it will do the right thing.
