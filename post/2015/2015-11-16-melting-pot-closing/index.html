Ann Arbor's The Melting Pot is closing

Nov 16, 2015

The Melting Pot in downtown Ann Arbor is closing as of today, November 16, 2015, according to a post on the restaurant’s web site and a story in MLive.

The last regular inspection of The Melting Pot by the Washtenaw County Health Department was on May 26, 2015. It turned up 11 violations, and some of them would be the sort of problems that would make you not want to go back. A copy of the inspection report makes for tough reading.

Observed the following: 1. Blackberries with mold like growth. 2. Mascapone: 2 - 3⁄27. 12 5⁄15. - Past date. 3. 3 butter milks 5⁄15. - Past date. 4. Prosciutto 10/13/2014.- Past date. 5. Two half & half 5⁄19.- Past date. 6. Two whole milks 5⁄5, 5⁄19.- Past date. 7. Bush mills whiskey had fly’s floating around in bottle. 8. Dewars white label scotch had fly’s floating around in bottle. 9. Cutty Sark Whisky had fly’s floating around in bottle. FOOD shall be safe, unADULTERATED, and, as specified under # 3-601.12, honestly presented. (P) To correct discard all noted items into the garbage. Corrected: Person in charge discarded all noted items into the garbage.

The restaurant should be inspected every 6 months, which means that their routine inspection should be in November 2015. I’ve sent a query to the county health deparment to determine if the restaurant was inspected yet this month, and if so what the results were.
